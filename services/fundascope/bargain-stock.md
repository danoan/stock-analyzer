To determine if a low EV/Revenue ratio truly indicates a bargain, consider the following factors:

1. **Revenue Growth**: Check if the company is experiencing steady or increasing revenue growth. A company with strong revenue growth might be undervalued if it's trading at a low EV/Revenue ratio.

2. **Profitability**: Look at the company's profit margins. If the company is already profitable or moving toward profitability, the low ratio might signal an undervaluation.

3. **Industry Comparison**: Compare the EV/Revenue ratio with those of similar companies in the industry. A low ratio could be more meaningful if the sector is healthy and the company performs well against its peers.

4. **Debt Levels**: Assess the company's capital structure. A high amount of debt might affect the enterprise value negatively but not always reflect operational performance. Ensuring the debt is manageable is crucial.

5. **Market Conditions**: Consider the broader economic and market conditions. A strong market position or brand can enhance a company's potential, despite a low ratio.

6. **Company Fundamentals**: Analyze other key metrics, such as earnings, cash flow, and return on investment. Strong fundamentals can support the argument that the company is a bargain.

7. **Qualitative Factors**: Look at management quality, competitive advantages, and industry trends. These factors can affect future growth prospects.Considering these aspects holistically will provide a more complete view and help confirm whether a low EV/Revenue signifies a genuine bargain.
